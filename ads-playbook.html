Step-by-Step: Build the Apprentice Pipeline Campaign

Follow these steps in Google Ads (ads.google.com). Click each step to expand.

1 Start a New Campaign ▼

Go to ads.google.com and click the blue "+" button or "New Campaign".

Select goal: Leads

Select conversion goal: Submit lead form

Campaign type: Search (NOT Performance Max - we want keyword control)

Uncheck "Include Google Display Network" to keep budget focused on search only.

2 Name & Budget ▼

Campaign name:

SMC - Apprentice Pipeline

Budget: $5.00/day

Bidding: Conversions (leave target CPA blank)

Location: United States

Language: English

3 Add Keywords ▼

Create an ad group named "Apprenticeship"

Paste ALL of these keywords at once:

"machinist apprenticeship" "CNC machinist training" "machine shop apprentice" "machinist career" "learn CNC machining" "trade school machining" "machinist jobs Atlanta"

The quotes mean phrase match - your ad shows when someone searches these phrases (or close variations). This prevents wasting budget on irrelevant searches.

4 Write the Ad ▼

Final URL:

https://southernmachinistcompany.com

Headline 1:

Machinist Apprenticeship

Headline 2:

Learn From 30yr Veterans

Headline 3:

Apply Today - Limited Spots

Description 1:

Start your career in precision machining. Hands-on training with experienced machinists. Apply now.

Description 2:

No experience needed. Learn CNC milling, turning, and more. Atlanta-based apprenticeship program.

Skip sitelinks and other optional stuff. Just fill the required fields above.

5 Publish & Verify ▼

Review everything looks right, then hit Publish.

Leave this one RUNNING (unlike Quote Seekers which we're keeping paused for now).

Google will review the ad (usually approved within a few hours).

Week 1 Optimization Playbook

After 7 days of running, we'll review performance and make these adjustments.

A Review Search Terms Report ▼

We'll pull the actual search terms people typed to trigger your ads via MCP.

Kill any irrelevant terms (add as negative keywords).

Double down on terms that got clicks/conversions.

This is where the MCP becomes the command center - I query, you act.

B Check Cost Per Lead ▼

Target: Under $20 per lead for quote requests, under $10 per lead for apprentice applications.

If cost per lead is too high: tighten keywords, pause low performers.

If cost per lead is good: increase budget on winners.

C A/B Test Ad Copy ▼

Create variant ads with different headlines/descriptions.

Test urgency ("Limited Spots" vs "Open Enrollment"), benefits ("Free Quote" vs "Same-Day Estimates"), authority ("30 Years" vs "Master Machinists").

We'll update this playbook with the winning copy for future campaigns.
